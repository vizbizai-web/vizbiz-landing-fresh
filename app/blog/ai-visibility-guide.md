## How AI Decides Who to Recommend

We spent six months reverse-engineering how AI makes these decisions. Here's what actually matters:

### 1. Entity Recognition
AI needs to know you exist as a distinct thing. Not just a website. A real dealership with a real location and real customers.

This means consistent Name, Address, Phone (NAP) across every directory, review site, and mention online. One mismatch and AI gets confused. Confused AI doesn't recommend you.

### 2. Citation Frequency
AI runs multiple searches before answering. "Best Toyota dealer." "Reliable mechanic." "Honest car sales."

The dealerships that appear across multiple contexts get recommended. The ones who only show up for "Toyota dealer [city]" get filtered out.

We call this RRF - Reciprocal Rank Fusion. It's the algorithm that decides who gets cited.

### 3. Review Sentiment
AI doesn't just count stars. It reads the words. "John was amazing" carries more weight than "Good experience 4/5."

Natural language processing means AI understands nuance. Specific praise beats generic ratings every time.

### 4. Contextual Relevance
AI matches query intent to business type. Someone asking about "oil change" gets service recommendations. Someone asking about "best deal" gets sales recommendations.

Your content needs to cover all contexts where you could be relevant.

### 5. Freshness
AI prefers active businesses. A website that hasn't been updated in two years signals a closed or struggling business.

Regular updates, new reviews, fresh content - these all signal that you're open and thriving.
